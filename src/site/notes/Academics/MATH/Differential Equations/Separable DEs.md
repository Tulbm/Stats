With functions in the form
$$
f(y) \frac{dy}{dt}=g(t)
$$
Integrating both sides with respect to t:
$$
\int f(y) \frac{dy}{dt}dt = \int g(t)dt \to \int f(y)dy = \int g(t)dt
$$
- by the change of variables theorem
So,
$$
\ln |A| = b + C \to \text{A} = ce^\text{b}
$$
